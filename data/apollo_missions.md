# Apollo Missions: Humanity's Journey to the Moon

## Overview

The Apollo program was NASA's third human spaceflight program, designed to land humans on the Moon and bring them safely back to Earth. It ran from 1961 to 1972 and remains one of humanity's greatest achievements.

## Apollo 11: The First Moon Landing

**Date:** July 16-24, 1969

Apollo 11 was the spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon. Commander Neil Armstrong and lunar module pilot Buzz Aldrin landed the Apollo Lunar Module Eagle on July 20, 1969, at 20:17 UTC. Armstrong became the first person to step onto the lunar surface six hours later on July 21 at 02:56 UTC.

**Famous Quote:** "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." - Neil Armstrong

**Crew Members:**
- Neil Armstrong (Commander)
- Buzz Aldrin (Lunar Module Pilot)
- Michael Collins (Command Module Pilot)

The mission duration was 8 days, 3 hours, 18 minutes, and 35 seconds. Armstrong and Aldrin spent about 21.5 hours on the lunar surface and collected 47.5 pounds (21.5 kg) of lunar material.

## Apollo 13: The Successful Failure

**Date:** April 11-17, 1970

Apollo 13 was intended to be the third mission to land on the Moon, but an oxygen tank explosion two days into the mission forced the crew to abort the lunar landing. Despite the life-threatening malfunction, the crew—Commander Jim Lovell, Command Module Pilot Jack Swigert, and Lunar Module Pilot Fred Haise—returned safely to Earth.

**Famous Quote:** "Houston, we've had a problem." - Jack Swigert

The mission is often called a "successful failure" because of the safe return of the crew despite the mission's inability to land on the Moon. The incident led to significant improvements in spacecraft design and mission planning.

## Apollo 17: The Final Moon Mission

**Date:** December 7-19, 1972

Apollo 17 was the final mission of NASA's Apollo program and remains the most recent time humans have traveled beyond low Earth orbit. Commander Eugene Cernan and Lunar Module Pilot Harrison Schmitt spent about 75 hours on the Moon in the Taurus-Littrow valley, while Command Module Pilot Ronald Evans orbited overhead.

**Key Achievements:**
- Longest Moon landing mission (12 days, 13 hours, 51 minutes)
- Longest total moonwalks (22 hours, 4 minutes)
- Largest lunar sample return (243.65 pounds or 110.52 kg)
- First scientist-astronaut to walk on the Moon (Harrison Schmitt, a geologist)

**Famous Quote:** "We leave as we came and, God willing, as we shall return, with peace and hope for all mankind." - Eugene Cernan (last words spoken on the Moon)

## Apollo Program Legacy

Between 1969 and 1972, six Apollo missions successfully landed 12 astronauts on the Moon. The program collected 842 pounds (382 kg) of lunar rocks and soil, which continue to be studied today. The technological innovations developed for Apollo have had lasting impacts on computing, telecommunications, and materials science.

The total cost of the Apollo program was approximately $25.4 billion (1973 dollars), equivalent to about $280 billion in 2023 dollars.
