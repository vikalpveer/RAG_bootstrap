# Planets of Our Solar System

## Inner Planets (Terrestrial Planets)

### Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It's only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 36 million miles (58 million km)
- Diameter: 3,032 miles (4,879 km)
- Day length: 59 Earth days
- Year length: 88 Earth days
- Moons: 0
- Surface temperature: -290°F to 800°F (-179°C to 427°C)

Mercury has a very thin atmosphere called an exosphere, composed mainly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. The planet's surface is heavily cratered, similar to Earth's Moon, due to billions of years of impacts.

### Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and Earth's closest planetary neighbor. It's often called Earth's "sister planet" due to similar size and mass, but conditions on Venus are extremely hostile.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 67 million miles (108 million km)
- Diameter: 7,521 miles (12,104 km)
- Day length: 243 Earth days (rotates backwards!)
- Year length: 225 Earth days
- Moons: 0
- Surface temperature: 900°F (475°C) - hot enough to melt lead

Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid. The atmospheric pressure on Venus is 92 times that of Earth. Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, meaning the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.

### Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. About 71% of Earth's surface is covered with water.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 93 million miles (150 million km)
- Diameter: 7,926 miles (12,756 km)
- Day length: 24 hours
- Year length: 365.25 days
- Moons: 1 (The Moon)
- Surface temperature: Average 59°F (15°C)

Earth's atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases. The planet has a strong magnetic field that protects it from harmful solar radiation.

### Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the solar system. Known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 142 million miles (228 million km)
- Diameter: 4,212 miles (6,779 km)
- Day length: 24.6 hours
- Year length: 687 Earth days
- Moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)
- Surface temperature: -195°F to 70°F (-125°C to 20°C)

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, which is about 13.6 miles (22 km) high—nearly three times the height of Mount Everest. The planet also has a canyon system called Valles Marineris that is over 2,500 miles (4,000 km) long.

## Outer Planets (Gas Giants and Ice Giants)

### Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in our solar system. It's a gas giant with a mass more than twice that of all the other planets combined.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 484 million miles (778 million km)
- Diameter: 86,881 miles (139,820 km)
- Day length: 10 hours
- Year length: 12 Earth years
- Moons: 95 confirmed moons (including the four large Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto)
- Atmosphere: Mainly hydrogen and helium

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging for at least 400 years. The storm is so large that three Earths could fit inside it. Jupiter has a faint ring system and the strongest magnetic field of any planet in the solar system.

### Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is famous for its spectacular ring system. It's the second-largest planet in our solar system.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 886 million miles (1.4 billion km)
- Diameter: 72,366 miles (116,464 km)
- Day length: 10.7 hours
- Year length: 29 Earth years
- Moons: 146 confirmed moons (including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solar system)
- Atmosphere: Mainly hydrogen and helium

Saturn's rings are made of billions of pieces of ice and rock, ranging in size from grains of sand to mountains. The rings extend up to 175,000 miles (282,000 km) from the planet but are only about 30 feet (10 meters) thick in most places.

### Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is unique because it rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of 98 degrees.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 1.8 billion miles (2.9 billion km)
- Diameter: 31,518 miles (50,724 km)
- Day length: 17 hours
- Year length: 84 Earth years
- Moons: 27 known moons
- Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane (which gives it a blue-green color)

Uranus is classified as an "ice giant" because its interior is composed mainly of ices and rock, unlike the gas giants Jupiter and Saturn. The planet's extreme tilt means that its poles experience 42 years of continuous sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness.

### Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system. It's the fourth-largest planet by diameter and the third-largest by mass.

**Key Facts:**
- Distance from Sun: 2.8 billion miles (4.5 billion km)
- Diameter: 30,599 miles (49,244 km)
- Day length: 16 hours
- Year length: 165 Earth years
- Moons: 16 known moons (including Triton, which orbits backwards)
- Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane

Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, with speeds reaching up to 1,200 mph (2,000 km/h). The planet appears deep blue due to methane in its atmosphere. Neptune was the first planet located through mathematical predictions rather than regular observations.
